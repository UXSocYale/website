Founded in Fall 2019, we're building Yale's design community & practice.

As a chapter of User Experience Society founded in Manila, We're designing Yale's space that works within product, design, engineering, and the arts to build experiences and products to shape Yale, New Haven, and beyond.

As a community, we're an application-based club that meets 1-2x a week during workshops and project-building. As an agency, we service everything from brand design, creative strategy, web engineering, and more.

We hold open workshops, design jams, and other events. We'd love to meet you sometime soon!

What's UXSociey going to look like this remote year?

We're crafting an asynchronous design curriculum for beginners, career development talks/workshops and recruiting season prep, and lots of work in designing for groups and organizations in need at Yale and beyond.


Our board is scattered all over the world right now, and we're lucky that UXSociety is positioned to work fantastically remotely. We're turning our synchronous meetings optional (but they'll still be fun opportunities for feedback, community events, and live co-learning), running asynchronous design education work, and more. Everything career-related/educational will be recorded and we have an intensely active Slack.

Synchronous needs will mostly be in small groups, such as for those in project teams with client meetings or in feedback sessions. We aim to be flexible, communicative, and here whenever you want to drop in for live feedback – or whether you want to learn at your own pace.
